Purpose and legal basis for processing personal information

We collect and process data about individuals solely on a consentual basis.

The main purpose for data collection is to provide the services as described on our web-site, and on any other marketing material. In short, we collect HR-related information about an organization's employees, process it, and serve it back to the organization that is the customer. In doing so we seek to help the organization improve their decision making around employee well-being.

Secondarily, we also use or will use anonymized data for statistical processing, for example in AI-models. This data may be shared with others through scientific publishing, blogging, or otherwise, but is always anonymized and to the best of our knowledge and ability, made completely untraceable.

Sharing of personal data

We may share ANOYMOUS data with third parties, never NON-ANONYMIZED data*. We do sometimes share NON-ANONYMIZED data between employer (customer) and employee (respondents). Cases where this may occur include, but are not limited to:

  • Cases where we explicity ask and receive consent to collect data that is meant to be shared, for example as preparation for a performance review.
  • Edge cases where a customer is able to extrapolate individual answers despite our reasonable effort to avoid this. For example given that all respondents give the lowest possible score to a question (lets say they all give a 1 on a scale from 1 to 5). The smart observer, noticing that the average for that question is 1, could reasonably deduce that the only way that happened was if everyone answered 1, and would thus know that each individual respondent gave a 1.
We seek to minimize the latter case, by providing extra layers of abstraction for small data sets. For example, we don't present data from single respondent surveys, and we only provide averages and computed scores for data sets with at least 4 respondents.

Transfer of NON-ANONYMOUS data out of the EU does as general rule not occur on our end, but our customers are free to bring data from our website with them, and customers may access our website and it's data from anywhere in the world so long as the proper login credentials are provided.

* We DO use cloud services to host our webpage and data. Though these have access to our data in a technical sense, we do not allow them to process or use such data for their own purposes. All our infrastructure is located in the EU to ensure these services are governed by the same strict privacy regulations that we are.

How long do we store data?

NON-ANONYMIZED personal data is generally stored for as long a company remains a customer, and for a period of up to five years after that. Companies may also delete an employee from our system, if for example, that employee quits. In such a case the data is immediatly anonymized. Individuals may also request their data be anonymized.

Users rights

Anyone whose data we store have the following rights:

  • The right to be informed about how we use their data. This information is kept and made avilable in this policy.
  • Right of access: We will share with WHAT data we have about you. In cases where you can be identified positively as the provider of the data, we will also share THE data we have about you. This distinction is neccessary, because we collect data with little validation as to who is providing the data.
  • Right of rectification: If we are holding erroneus data about you, you may ask for it to be rectified.
  • Right to erasure: We will erase your NON-ANONYMIZED data at your request.
  • Right to restrict processing: You may ask that your NON-ANONYMIZED data not be processed in our systems.
  • Right to data portability: You have the right to obtain and reuse data you have shared with us with third parties.
  • Right to object to use of your data for direct marketing, or processing of your personal information where the basis is lawful interest : We do not use data in this manner.
  • Rights in relation to automated decision making and profiling : We do not use data in this manner.
You have a right to raise any complaint about your rights to the data protection agency in your country.
